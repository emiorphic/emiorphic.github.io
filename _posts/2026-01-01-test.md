It is actually possible to derive conservation of mechanical energy in a rather elementary way. We need only three axioms and basic calculus. 

# Setup
Consider a system of $$N$$ particles, indexed by $$i$$, with masses $$m_i$$. We assume the following three axioms:
1. **Newton's Second Law:**

    $$
    \begin{equation} \label{eq:newton2}
    m_i \vb{a}_i = \sum_{\substack{1 \leq j \leq N \\ j \neq i}} \vb{F}_{ij}.
    \end{equation}
    $$
2. **Newton's Third Law:**

    $$
    \begin{equation} \label{eq:newton3}
    \vb{F}_{ij} = -\vb{F}_{ji}.
    \end{equation}
    $$
3. **Conservative Central Forces:**

    $$
    \begin{equation} \label{eq:concf}
    \vb{F}_{ij} = -u'(r_{ij}) \vu{r}_{ij},
    \end{equation}
    $$
    
    where $$r_{ij} = \norm{\vb{r}_{ij}}$$ and $$u(r_{ij})$$ is the pair potential.

Next, we define the total mechanical energy $$E = T + U$$ of the system as the sum of kinetic and potential energy $$T$$ and $$U$$ respectively, where

$$
T = \sum_{1 \leq i \leq N} \frac{1}{2} m_i (\vb{v}_i \dotproduct \vb{v}_i) \quad \text{and} \quad U = \sum_{i \leq i < j \leq N} u(r_{ij}).
$$

Our goal is to prove that the quantity $$E$$ is conserved, i.e.

$$
\dv{E}{t} = 0.
$$

# Derivation
We differentiate $$T$$ with respect to $$t$$:

$$
\begin{align*}
    \dv{T}{t} &= \sum_{1 \leq i \leq N} \frac{1}{2} m_i \dv{t} (\vb{v}_i \dotproduct \vb{v}_i)\\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i \leq N} m_i (\vb{v}_i \dotproduct \vb{a}_i) \qquad \left(\dv{t} (\vb{v} \dotproduct \vb{v}) = 2 \vb{v} \dotproduct \dv{\vb{v}}{t} = 2 (\vb{v} \dotproduct \vb{a})\right)\\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i \leq N} \vb{v}_i \dotproduct (m_i \vb{a}_i) \\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i \leq N} \vb{v}_i \dotproduct \sum_{\substack{1 \leq j \leq N \\ j \neq i}} \vb{F}_{ij} \\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} \vb{v}_i \dotproduct \vb{F}_{ij} + \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} \vb{v}_j \dotproduct \vb{F}_{ji} \\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} \vb{v}_i \dotproduct \vb{F}_{ij} + \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} \vb{v}_j \dotproduct (-\vb{F}_{ij})\\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} \vb{v}_{ij} \dotproduct \vb{F}_{ij} \qquad \left(\vb{v}_{ij} = \vb{v}_i - \vb{v}_j\right)
\end{align*}
$$

Next, we differentiate $$U$$ with respect to $$t$$ as well:

$$
\begin{align*}
    \dv{U}{t} &= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} u'(r_{ij}) \dv{r_{ij}}{t}\\
    &= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq N} u'(r_{ij}) \frac{\vb{r}_{ij} \dotproduct \vb{v}_{ij}}{r_{ij}}
\end{align*}
$$
