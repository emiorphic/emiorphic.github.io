It is actually possible to derive conservation of mechanical energy in a rather elementary way. We need only three axioms and basic calculus. 

# Setup
Consider a system of $$N$$ particles, indexed by $$i$$, with masses $$m_i$$. We assume the following three axioms:
1. **Newton's Second Law:**

    $$
    m_i \vb{a}_i = \sum_{j=1, j \neq i}^{N} \vb{F}_{ij}.
    $$
2. **Newton's Third Law:**

    $$
    \vb{F}_{ij} = -\vb{F}_{ji}.
    $$
3. **Conservative Central Forces:**

    $$
    \vb{F}_{ij} = -u'(r_{ij}) \frac{\vb{r}_i - \vb{r}_j}{r_{ij}},
    $$
    where $$r_{ij} = \norm{\vb{r}_{i}-\vb{r}_{j}}$$ and $$u(r_{ij})$$ is the pair potential.
